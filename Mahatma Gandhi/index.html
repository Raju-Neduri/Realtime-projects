Early Life

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on 2 October 1869 in Porbandar, a coastal town in present-day Gujarat, India. His father, Karamchand Gandhi, was a chief minister of Porbandar and his mother, Putlibai, was a devout Hindu.

Philosophy

Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolent resistance, which he called Satyagraha, was influenced by his Hindu faith and his belief in ahimsa (nonviolence). He used nonviolent civil disobedience to challenge British rule in India and to advocate for the rights of the Indian people.

One of his most famous quotes is "Be the change you wish to see in the world."

Achievements

  • Leading India to independence from British rule
  • Promoting Hindu-Muslim unity
  • Championing the rights of the lower castes and the untouchables
  • Advocating for women's rights
